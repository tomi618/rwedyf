Hajia (Chief) Maryam Ladi Ibrahim, FCNA, born in 1952 in Adavi, Kogi State, is a trailblazing Nigerian accountant, public servant, and philanthropist. A former President/Chairman of the Association of National Accountants of Nigeria (ANAN), she was the first woman in Nigeria to serve as a State Auditor-General. With a strong academic background from Ahmadu Bello University and numerous international certifications, she has held various influential positions in public service, including Secretary to the Kogi State Government and pioneer Director General of the Kogi State Lands and Housing Services Bureau. She is also the founder of the Rural Women Empowerment and Youth Development Foundation (RWEYDF), a prominent NGO supporting women and youth through education, micro-credit, and entrepreneurship. An award-winning leader and advocate for transparency, peace, and girl-child education, she has contributed extensively to national development through roles in organizations such as FRCN, NEITI, PAFA, and ABWA.

Our Mission:

We exist to empower rural women and youth by providing them with the skills, resources, and opportunities they need to lead healthy, dignified, and economically productive lives. Through a combination of health interventions, educational support, micro-credit schemes, and advocacy for human and gender rights, we work to remove systemic barriers and help individuals transform their aspirations into lasting change.

Our Approach:

At RWEYDF, we believe that true empowerment starts at the grassroots. We work hand-in-hand with local communities, listening to their unique challenges and co-creating solutions that are practical and sustainable. Our programs are grounded in compassion, equity, and evidence-based practices.

We actively collaborate with:

  • Government agencies
  • Civil society organizations (CSOs)
  • Health institutions and international donors
This collaborative spirit ensures that our projects are not only impactful but also scalable and sustainable.
